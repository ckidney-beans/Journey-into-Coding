## A Middle-Aged ADHD Adventure into Programming

Hello there, fellow internet wanderer! You've stumbled upon my digital garden—a living, growing collection of my journey into the wonderful (and occasionally terrifying) world of coding. This isn't one of those perfectly manicured gardens with everything in its right place. It's more like that community garden plot where sometimes things flourish unexpectedly and other times the squirrels make off with your prized tomatoes.

### Why Am I Doing This?

Great question! As a middle-aged person with ADHD, I've discovered that:

1. My brain craves new challenges
2. I forget things approximately 37 seconds after learning them
3. Public accountability keeps me on track

This site serves as both my learning journal and my accountability buddy. If I don't update it, feel free to imagine me staring at a blinking cursor somewhere, completely distracted by researching the perfect code editor theme instead of actually coding.

### The Grand Master Plan

**Short-term Goal:**
Become decent with Python—decent enough to write basic to intermediate programs without having to Google "how to print hello world" every time. (No judgment if you do this too.)

**Long-term Goal:**
Conquer the full stack of web development and design, eventually building this very site from scratch. Yes, that means one day this Jekyll/GitHub Pages setup will be replaced by my own creation. (Future me, if you're reading this and still haven't done it, GET BACK TO WORK.)

### My Learning Arsenal

I'm arming myself with these excellent resources:

* **Code with Mosh** - Where I nod along confidently until I realize I have no idea what just happened
* **Code Wars** - Where I experience the full emotional range from "I am a coding genius" to "I should never be allowed near a computer again" in 20-minute intervals
* **CSS Battle** - Where I discover that moving something 2 pixels to the left takes 47 lines of code and a sacrifice to the styling gods
* **Various documentation sites** - Where I pretend to understand what I'm reading

### What You'll Find Here

This garden will grow to include:
* Project showcases (bugs and all)
* Learning notes (with occasional coffee stains)
* Coding victories (no matter how small)
* Debugging war stories
* Resources that have helped me
* Probably some memes when the coding gets tough

### The Journey So Far

Currently, I'm in the "everything is both exciting and overwhelming" phase. Check back regularly to witness the thrilling progression to "I might actually know what I'm doing" and hopefully not too much of the "I've broken everything and don't know why" stage.

### Join Me?

Whether you're a seasoned developer who wants to watch this journey with amusement, a fellow learner looking for solidarity, or just my mom checking to see if I'm sticking with my new hobby (hi Mom!), feel free to follow along.

Remember: Gardens grow with time, attention, and the occasional desperate AI searches for debugging help.
